# 02 — Branding & Token

## Names

- **Game:** GRINDSET
- **Token:** `$GRIND` (SPL, Solana)
- **Tagline:** *Touch grass? Mine it.*
- **Domains:** grindset.xyz, playgrindset.com (reserve)

## Voice & tone

Ironic, self-aware, terminally online. Leans into grind-culture and crypto memes without cringe. Think: Postie Pete rewritten by a crypto-Twitter shitposter. Serious *enough* that the economy works; meme-heavy *enough* that it spreads.

- We say: grind, mire, toil, sigma, ngmi, bag, rug (ironic)
- We do not say: "investment opportunity," "guaranteed yield," "earn money playing," "revolutionary"
- Legal-safe framing: utility token for in-game economic activity

## Logo concept

Pixelated pickaxe crossed with a candlestick chart, both forming an "X" inside a shield. The shield's border is a green up-wick on the left, red down-wick on the right. Monochrome-safe; works as a 16×16 favicon and a 4K banner. The wordmark "GRINDSET" is chunky blackletter-meets-pixel — fantasy roots, digital edges.

## Typography

- **Display / wordmark:** chunky pixel-blackletter hybrid (custom or VT323 heavy weight)
- **UI:** Inter or Geist
- **Numbers / prices:** JetBrains Mono (tabular)

## Why this hits viral

- "GRINDSET" is already a meme ecosystem (sigma, hustle, 4am alarms). Free cultural gravity.
- `$GRIND` is self-describing and easy to chant.
- The grind loop maps cleanly to shitposting: "125 hours to max mining" is a tweet format.
- Self-deprecation is an immune response to the "crypto game = scam" reflex.
